# Hieroglyphic Cosmos: Curated List of 100 Important Egyptian Hieroglyphs

This list curates 100 Egyptian hieroglyphs for the "Hieroglyphic Cosmos" project, mapping them to cosmic and astronomical themes in museum-quality art. Selection prioritizes cultural/religious/historical significance, visual distinctiveness, cosmic resonance, and balance between popular (e.g., Ankh) and lesser-known glyphs. Choices draw from Gardiner's sign list, favoring symbols evoking divinity, celestial cycles, creation, eternity, and universal order for scholarly and artistic use. In the Gardiner Code column, "(V)" indicates a variant form of the primary sign.

### Notes on Variants
- **C22 (V)**: God with moon (Khonsu) is a variant of divine figures with specific attributes, differing from standard god signs like C1.
- **I14 (V)**: Ouroboros is a variant of serpent signs (e.g., I1), emphasizing the cyclical tail-biting form.
- **N35 (V)**: Constellation is a variant of the water ripple (N35), used here in a rare stellar context.
- **D63 (V)**: Shadow is a variant of human-related signs, distinct for its ethereal depiction.
- **N39 (V)**: Void is a variant of pool/land signs, used uniquely for emptiness in this context.
