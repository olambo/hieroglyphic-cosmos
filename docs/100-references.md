# Hieroglyphic Cosmos: Top 100 Hieroglyphs Selection

**Purpose:**  
This document provides a curated list of 100 hieroglyphs prioritized for the Hieroglyphic Cosmos project. Selection is based on:
- **Astronomical relevance:** Symbols connected to stars, planets, sky, and celestial events.  
- **Mythological importance:** Deities, sacred animals, and symbols central to Egyptian cosmology.  
- **Visual impact:** Distinctive and iconic glyphs for large-scale artwork.  
- **Cultural significance:** Hieroglyphs that convey key concepts like life, death, power, time, and eternity.  

**Methodology Summary:**  
- Reference: Gardiner's Sign List, extended with astronomical and mythological variants.  
- Categories prioritized: Sky, stars, sun, moon, planets, gods, sacred animals, cosmological concepts.  
- Aim: balance between recognizability for general audiences and symbolic richness for scholars.

**Summary of Reasoning:**  
- The selection prioritizes **astronomy-related symbols** (stars, sun, moon, barques, falcons) for cosmic mapping.  
- Includes **major deities** (Osiris, Isis, Horus, etc.) and sacred animals (falcon, ibis, lion, cat) for mythological significance.  
- Focus on **life, death, and eternity** (Ankh, Djed, Shen ring, Ka, Ba, Maat) to anchor the cosmic narrative.  
- Timekeeping and calendrical symbols are included to illustrate cycles, rhythms, and cosmic order.  
- Ritual and magic glyphs enhance visual richness and symbolic depth for museum audiences.  
- Visual impact was considered for large-scale artwork; iconic and dynamic shapes were prioritized for legibility and artistic resonance.  

*This curated list balances historical authenticity, symbolic richness, and aesthetic value for the Hieroglyphic Cosmos project.*
